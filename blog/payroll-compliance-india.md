## Why This Matters More Than You Think

I've been processing payroll for 60+ employees at Kankatala Textiles with 100% HRMS accuracy, and I'll tell you directly: the cost of a single missed PF challan — interest at 12% p.a. plus damages up to 25% of arrears — almost always exceeds the monthly cost of having a dedicated payroll person.

Most SMEs don't miss deadlines because they don't care. They miss them because nobody owns a written calendar. This guide fixes that.

## The Four Pillars

### Provident Fund (PF) — EPFO

**Applicable to:** Organisations with 20+ employees (voluntary for smaller)

**Contribution structure:**
- Employee: **12%** of Basic + DA
- Employer: **12%** split — 3.67% to EPF, 8.33% to EPS (Employees' Pension Scheme)

**Critical deadlines:**
- Monthly ECR (Electronic Challan cum Return): **15th of following month**
- Annual returns (Form 3A / 6A): **30 April**
- New employee registration: **Within 1 month of joining**

**GreytHR tip:** Set the system reminder 5 days before the 15th, not on the 14th. Leave a buffer for bank transaction delays.

---

### Employee State Insurance (ESI) — ESIC

**Applicable to:** Employees earning ≤ ₹21,000/month; organisations with 10+ employees (20 in some states)

**Contribution rates:**
- Employee: **0.75%** of gross wages
- Employer: **3.25%** of gross wages

**Critical deadlines:**
- Monthly challan: **15th of following month**
- Half-yearly return: **11 May** (Oct–Mar) and **11 November** (Apr–Sep)
- New employee registration: **Within 10 days of joining** ← this one gets missed constantly

**Common mistake:** Not registering new joiners within 10 days. This creates a benefit gap where the employee isn't covered, and the employer has no documentary protection if a workplace injury occurs.

---

### Professional Tax (PT) — State-specific

**Andhra Pradesh (where I operate):**
- Employees earning > ₹15,000/month: **₹200/month** deducted from salary
- Monthly remittance: **10th of the following month**
- Annual return: **10 April**

**Multi-state reminder:** If your organisation has employees in multiple states, PT rates and income slabs differ. Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu all have different structures. Maintain a state-wise tracker.

---

### Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) — Section 192

**Applicable to:** Any employer paying salary above the basic exemption limit

**Critical deadlines:**
- Monthly TDS deposit: **7th of following month** (Government deductors: same day)
- Quarterly returns (Form 24Q): 31 Jul · 31 Oct · 31 Jan · 31 May
- Form 16 to employees: **15 June** of the assessment year

**Year-start action:** Collect **Form 12BB** from all employees in April. This captures investment declarations (HRA, 80C, 80D, etc.) and prevents year-end tax shock.

## The 3 Mistakes I See Most Often in SMEs

**1. Treating increments as retroactive without updating TDS**
When an employee gets a salary hike mid-year, TDS must be recalculated from the date of effect — not just going forward. Missing this leads to shortfall notices in Form 16.

**2. Not reconciling HRMS data against bank statements monthly**
I run a 3-way reconciliation every month: HRMS payroll output → bank payment file → statutory challan amounts. Any discrepancy surfaces immediately.

**3. Ignoring PT slab changes in April**
Some states revise PT rates annually. Build a calendar reminder for the first week of April to verify the current slabs for every state you operate in.
