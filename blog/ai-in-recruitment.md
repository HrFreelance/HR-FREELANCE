## The Certification Changed How I Think

When I completed the **Generative AI, Recruiting, and Talent Acquisition** certification on LinkedIn, I expected to learn about tools. What I actually learned was a mental model: AI is a *force multiplier for your judgment*, not a replacement for it.

That mental model was immediately tested at ComTec, where I was doing US IT staffing and needed to maintain an 85% offer acceptance rate while screening at scale. Here's the honest account of what worked.

## What AI Actually Accelerated

### Boolean String Construction

Before AI: generating a precise Boolean search for a niche US IT role (say, a Kubernetes engineer with Terraform and Azure experience) took 15–20 minutes if done carefully. With AI prompting, I could generate, test, and refine strings in under 3 minutes. The improvement wasn't marginal — it was a genuine workflow transformation.

My prompt formula:
> *"Generate a LinkedIn Boolean search string for a [ROLE] with mandatory skills [A, B, C], nice-to-have [D, E], excluding [staffing agencies / students]. Optimise for India-based profiles targeting US roles."*

### Job Description Drafting

Generic JDs attract generic candidates. That's the data. AI let me customise every JD for the specific hiring manager's communication style, the team's pain points, and the market salary band — in one-fifth the time.

> The best JD I ever wrote (80% interview-to-hire on that role) was co-authored with ChatGPT, but every constraint — the team culture, the non-negotiables, the red flags — came from a 20-minute conversation with the hiring manager.

### First-Round Screening Questions

Consistency matters at scale. AI helped me draft role-specific technical and behavioural screening questions, which I then reviewed and personalised. This meant my 150+ screening calls at ComTec had a structured baseline, making comparison genuinely fair.

## What AI Cannot Do

- **Read hesitation on a call.** When a candidate pauses before answering a comp question, that tells you something an algorithm will never surface.
- **Negotiate gracefully.** My 85% offer acceptance rate came from *listening* — understanding what each candidate actually wanted beyond the number.
- **Assess culture fit.** Every team has an unwritten social contract. Evaluating fit requires domain knowledge of the team, not keyword matching.
- **BGV conversations.** Background verification edge cases require human judgment and legal awareness.

## The Risk: Bias Amplification

This is the issue most recruiters don't talk about openly. If your AI tool is trained on historical hiring data that skewed toward certain educational backgrounds or geographies, it will systematically de-prioritise diverse candidates. 

My practice: I use AI for the top-of-funnel (sourcing and initial screening questions), but the shortlist review is always done by a human who is explicitly checking for demographic diversity in the pool.

## A Practical Workflow

1. **AI generates** JD, Boolean strings, and screening question bank
2. **Human reviews** all AI outputs before use
3. **AI supports** scheduling automation (Calendly integrations)
4. **Human leads** all screening calls, assessments, and offer negotiations
5. **AI helps** with post-hire analytics and pipeline reporting

## The ChatGPT in MS Office Layer

I also completed the **ChatGPT & AI in Microsoft Office** certification through Skill Nation. The practical application: using Copilot-style prompts in Excel to build recruitment dashboards, and in Word to automate offer letter templates. These are small efficiency gains but they compound.

## Bottom Line

The recruiters winning in 2025 aren't those who *avoid* AI or those who *outsource everything* to it. They're the ones who use AI to clear the low-value volume so they can do the high-value relationship work better.
